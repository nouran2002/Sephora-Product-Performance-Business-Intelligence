# Sephora Beauty Product Performance Analysis

## Overview 
This case study analyzes product performance data from **Sephoraâ€™s online retail catalog** to identify key revenue drivers, customer engagement patterns, and rating behavior. 
The goal is to provide data-driven insights that support better product selection, premium pricing strategies, and targeted marketing decisions for the beauty industry leader.

## Business Scenario
Sephora offers a wide range of beauty products including skincare, makeup, fragrances, and personal care items.  
The company aims to improve revenue performance and customer satisfaction by analyzing product pricing, customer ratings, reviews, and engagement metrics.

## Business Problem
Sephora needs better visibility into which products, brands, and categories drive the highest revenue and customer engagement.  
Without these insights, it is challenging to optimize product assortment, pricing strategies, and promotional efforts.

## Dataset
- Source: Sephora product dataset(from Kaggle)
- The dataset includes product information such as price, customer ratings, number of reviews, likes (loves_count), and product categories.
- A cleaned version of the dataset is used for analysis.

## Data Cleaning
Data cleaning and preparation were performed using Python(Pandas), including:
- Selecting relevant product and brand-related columns
- Handling missing values in ratings and categories
- Removing duplicate records
- Creating a revenue metric based on product price and number of reviews
- Saving a cleaned dataset for further analysis

The full data cleaning process is documented in the Python notebook:
`data_cleaning.ipynb`
